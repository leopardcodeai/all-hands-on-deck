Privacy Policy

All Hands On Deck · Last updated: 2026-05-01

This policy describes what data the All Hands On Deck app and web viewer handle, and why. The app is built around ephemeral group-photo sessions: there are no accounts, no persistent profiles, and no long-term storage. Whenever possible, data stays on your device.

1. Who we are

All Hands On Deck is operated by Alexander Brunker (contact: alexander.brunker@gmail.com). See the Imprint page for the full legal address.

2. What data we process

  • Camera frames & final photos. While a session is live, low-resolution preview frames (~640px JPEG) and the captured group photo are relayed through Supabase Realtime/Storage or peer-to-peer WebRTC DataChannels to other participants of the same session. They are never written to long-term storage on our side and are removed when the session expires (TTL, typically under 1 hour).
  • Session ID + display name. Each session has a random 6–16 character ID and the participants' chosen display names (which can be a pseudonym such as a Game Center alias or a pirate rank). Both are transmitted to other session participants.
  • Local device data. The iOS app stores user preferences (language, grid/level toggles, web-join default) in Apple's UserDefaults on your device. This data never leaves the device.
  • Network metadata. Supabase and the hosting provider log IP addresses and timestamps for the duration the connection lasts, as is standard for any internet service. We do not link IP addresses to identities.
  • Crash diagnostics. If you opt in via iOS Settings (Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics), Apple may share crash reports with us. These are anonymous.

3. What data we do NOT collect

  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA), no tracking SDKs, no analytics SDK.
  • No persistent user accounts, no email or phone collection.
  • No location data.
  • No persistent chat or message history outside the live session.

4. Legal basis (GDPR)

The lawful basis for processing the camera-frame and session data above is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — performance of the service you requested by joining or hosting a session. No further processing takes place after the session ends.

5. Sub-processors

We use Supabase (Postgres, Realtime, Storage, Auth) as a sub-processor for session data and photo storage. Supabase processes data in line with its privacy documentation.

6. Your rights

Under GDPR you have the right to access, correct, and request deletion of personal data we hold. Because the service is account-less and ephemeral, in practice we have no data to return or delete after a session ends. For any concerns, contact alexander.brunker@gmail.com. You may also lodge a complaint with the data-protection authority of your residence.

7. Changes

Material changes to this policy will be reflected in the "Last updated" date and announced in the app where reasonable.
